Guideline Title: CDC Pediatric Fever Triage Criteria
Source Citation: CDC Clinical Guidance for Infant and Pediatric Fever Management (2024)
Key Criteria:
1. Infants under 3 months (90 days) with a rectal temperature of 100.4°F (38°C) or higher must be triaged as HIGH urgency. They require immediate medical evaluation, septic workup, and monitoring due to the risk of serious bacterial infections (SBI).
2. Children aged 3 to 36 months with a temperature of 102.2°F (39°C) or higher should be triaged as MEDIUM urgency. They require clinical evaluation to identify the source of infection, monitor hydration, and review immunization history.
3. Children presenting with fever accompanied by lethargy, inconsolable crying, toxic appearance, petechial rash, or stiff neck must be triaged as HIGH urgency regardless of age.
4. Simple viral fevers with temperature under 101.5°F (38.6°C) in fully immunized children who are active and hydrated are triaged as LOW urgency. Provide symptomatic care instructions (hydration, Acetaminophen/Ibuprofen).
